Why I Love Using Express For My Backend

By Jacob S. Carbonell · January 15, 2026 · 5 min read

The E in Backend

Did you know that the “E” in “backend” stands for Express? Well, that may not be true for you, dear readers, but it is the absolute truth for me! If you are looking for someone to convince you why Express is the best tool for your backend and encourage you to use it, then you are in the right place. In this blog, I will share how much I love Express and why I use it. I am confident that you will enjoy it as much as I do!

If you are a programmer who loves backend development like me, chances are you have heard about Express at some point in your life. But for those who have never heard of it, Express is a very well known Node.js web application framework today. Why? Because it is easy, well-documented, and unopinionated, which means that it does not follow a strict set of rules or standard, you are free to use it however you like.


When It All Started

When I first started coding, I was curious about how web applications worked under the hood. I thought that they all just ran in the browser. But I was actually wrong. I learned that there are layers and layers of abstraction that make it possible for things to be displayed on the screen. At a high level, most web pages get their data from outside sources running on separate computers. These computers are called servers, and they can use entirely different programming languages to serve information to the client. In this case, Express uses JavaScript, a programming language I use the most.

When I learned about this, I was so happy because I wasn’t expecting it to be this easy and intuitive to learn. I realized that there is so much I can do with Express to meet my needs, and that my system doesn’t have to be complex for it to deliver what I want. Ever since then, I have used Express for all of my web projects, without even considering other technologies. It gives me all the control I need: no unnecessary files and folders, easy management of routes and endpoints, readable syntax, and much more to make my workflow quicker and more efficient. It helps me prioritize the problem at hand instead of deviating my focus in configuring the tools needed to work on it.

Express is also very well-documented. Whenever I encounter an issue, all it takes to solve it is a simple Google search. This taught me the value of using well-established technologies with a large user base to make debugging and seeking help much easier. This really made me appreciate the essence of teamwork and collaboration in software development, because coding is not always a thing that you do all by yourself. Everything that I use, from the programming languages and software to the frameworks and tools, are created and maintained by other people. Therefore, I should always try to participate in the community of these technologies, such as Express.


Conclusion

Overall, Express has taught me more than just how to build a backend, it has shown me the importance of simplicity, well-documented code, and working with tools that align with my strengths. I’ve realized that choosing technologies with good documentation and a strong community isn’t just convenient, it shapes the way I approach problems, learn new concepts, and build maintainable systems. Looking back, Express didn’t just make development easier, it helped me grow as a backend developer, giving me the confidence to experiment, explore, and create without feeling overwhelmed. For anyone interested in starting their journey into this kind of development, I believe Express offers not just a framework, but a way to understand the bigger picture of how web applications truly work.
